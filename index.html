About me

A counselor who's been inside the system

I'm Wonny — a Certified Rehabilitation Counselor (CRC) based in Gangneung, with a background that spans Korean government welfare institutions, U.S. vocational rehabilitation practice, and front-line international student support.

I spent four years working inside Korea's Workers' Compensation and Welfare system, gaining a deep understanding of how institutional support structures actually operate — not just on paper, but in practice.

I then earned my M.S. in Rehabilitation Counseling from the University of Wisconsin-Stout, and went on to work for two years as a Vocational Coordinator at Aurora Vocational Services in the U.S. There, I collaborated directly with DVR (Division of Vocational Rehabilitation) — a federal-state program supporting individuals with disabilities toward employment — and delivered IPS (Individual Placement and Support), an evidence-based model helping people facing mental health barriers find and sustain competitive, integrated employment. Across both systems, I worked closely with clients navigating complex, overlapping barriers: disability, language, cultural transition, and socioeconomic challenges.

Back in Korea, I now work as a consultant for international students. I also speak Vietnamese, which allows me to serve one of Korea's largest and most underserved immigrant communities directly.

My approach is evidence-based but deeply human. I write about vocational counseling, disability employment, and career navigation — because good information, shared openly, changes people's lives.
